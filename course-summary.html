/ Identities & Inclusion / Summary
Course Complete

Social identities include things like race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, sex, ability, socioeconomic status, and size. Society treats us differently depending on these identities, whether they be real or perceived.

We are likely all guilty of stereotyping, having biases, or committing microaggressions. Going forward, try to catch yourself if you do any of these things and challenge yourself to think differently moving forward. We all play a role in creating a safe and inclusive environment for our friends, peers, and classmates.

Key Takeaways
  • Social identities are social constructs.
  • We all have multiple social identities.
  • Some social identities are more fixed than others.
  • People categorize us based on our social identities.
