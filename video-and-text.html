It’s also important to know that someone can’t give consent if they are incapacitated due to drugs or alcohol, under the legal age, or being coerced or forced.

Asking for consent doesn’t have to be awkward. There are many ways to ensure that everyone is on the same page about engaging in sexual activity. For example, you can ask “is this okay?” or “tell me what you want me to do to you” or “do you want me to ….”. You can also give consent by saying “I want you to…” or by asking someone to touch you in a certain way.

If someone says no, is unable to give consent, or even doesn’t give a clear or enthusiastic yes, you must stop. Open up communication and talk about your boundaries. Never assume that someone is going to be into what you’re into or ready when you’re ready.

These conversations are really important to have. In most cases, sexual assault is committed by someone the victim knows. Although a lot of the cases we hear about on the news are committed by strangers, the reality is that most assaults are committed by non-strangers. We can help protect our community by talking to each other about consent and sexual assault, what behaviors are acceptable and not acceptable, and the consequences of committing an act of sexual violence.
